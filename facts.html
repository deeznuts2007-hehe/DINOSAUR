Amazing Dinosaur Facts

Did you know that the word "dinosaur" means "terrible lizard" in Greek?
The largest dinosaur ever discovered is Argentinosaurus, which could reach over 100 feet long.
Velociraptors were actually much smaller in real life than depicted in movies, standing only about 3 feet tall.
Some dinosaurs had feathers, suggesting they were closely related to modern birds.
The T-Rex had one of the strongest bite forces of any land animal, capable of crushing bones.
Triceratops had up to 800 teeth, which were continuously replaced throughout its life.
Spinosaurus was one of the only known dinosaurs that likely lived a semi-aquatic lifestyle.
Pterosaurs, often mistaken for dinosaurs, were actually flying reptiles and not true dinosaurs.
Fossilized dinosaur eggs have been found with preserved embryos inside, helping scientists understand their growth.
The asteroid that led to the extinction of the dinosaurs struck near present-day Mexico about 66 million years ago.
Some dinosaurs, like the Parasaurolophus, had crests on their heads that may have been used to produce sounds.
Stegosaurus had bony plates along its back that may have helped regulate its body temperature.
Many dinosaurs, including the Brachiosaurus, had hollow bones, making them lighter and more agile.
Dinosaurs lived during the Mesozoic Era, which lasted for about 180 million years.
Fossilized footprints of dinosaurs, called trackways, help scientists learn about their movement and behavior.
